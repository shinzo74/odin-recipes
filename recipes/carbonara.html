Spaghetti Carbonara

Description

Carbonara is an Italian pasta dish from Rome made with eggs, hard cheese, cured pork, and black pepper. The dish arrived at its modern form, with its current name, in the middle of the 20th century.

The dish is documented as long ago as 1839. Since the name is derived from carbonaro (italian for charcoal burner some believe the dish was first made as a hearty meal for Italian charcoal workers.

Ingridients:

  • 100g pancetta
  • 50g pecorino cheese
  • 3 large eggs
  • 250g spaghetti
  • 2 tsp Oil of Olive
  • sea salt and black pepper

Steps:

  1. Step 1

    Heat olive oilin a pan, add guanciale. Cook, turning occasionally, until evenly browned and crispy, 5 to 10 minutes.

  2. Step 2

    Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti in the boiling water, about 9 minutes.

  3. Step 3

    Pour pasta in guanciale pan, lightly cook and stir, add whisked egg and pecorino cheese while stirring.

  4. Step 4

    Add Pepper and Eat. Buon Appetito!
